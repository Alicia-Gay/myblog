Want to DIY?

  • Eyewear Chain

    • Eyeglass chains are practical accessories, but most commercial versions are cheaply made, generic or very expensive. Fortunately, you can design and make your own one-of-a-kind beaded eyeglass chain that doubles as a necklace. It's so easy!

Materials

49-strand nylon coated flexible bead stringing wire (such as Soft Flex)

Two clamshell bead tips, preferably sterling silver

Four thick-walled 2 mm crimp tubes

Seed beads

Two clasps—lobster claw, trigger or spring ring type

Sturdy open jump rings

A matching closed jump ring

Two eyeglass holder ends

Tools

Flocked bead board

Measuring tape

Wire pearl reamer

Flush cutter, side cutter or wire nipper

Crimping pliers

Crimping pliers

Round-nose pliers

Two pairs of chain-nose pliers

Steps

Step 1: Cut the Beading Wire to Length

Step 2: Design Your Convertible Eyeglass Chain/Necklace

Step 3: Add a Clamshell Bead Tip Onto One End of the Beading Wire

Step 4: Add the First Crimp Tube

Step 5: Add a Large-Holed Spacer Bead and Another Crimp

Step 6: Trim the Beading Wire

Step 7: String the Remaining Beads, Crimp Tubes, and Clamshell Bead Tip

Step 8: Close the Clamshell and Trim the Wire

Step 9: Attach the Clasps to the Bead Tips

Step 10: Make the Eyeglass Holder Adapters
